About Us

Lewiston Monthly Meeting is a small, un-programmed meeting that meets on Sunday mornings, September through June allowing us opportunities for inter-visitation during the summer months. We are currently meeting at 161 Wood Street in a building on the Bates campus. We wait in silent worship to be moved by God to offer vocal ministry. Sometimes someone comes with a prayer, poem or short reading to give at the beginning of worship; sometimes we sing to start worship.

There have been Quakers in Lewiston for over 200 years, although not a continuously active meeting. Our current meeting began in the 1970s as a worship group that met in homes and was under the care of Durham Friends Meeting. It became a monthly meeting in 1980. In addition to our weekly meetings for worship, we meet to conduct business once a month.

Lewiston Monthly Meeting, like other Quaker Meetings in southern Maine, is part of Falmouth Quarterly Meeting, which meets three times a year for business. Lewiston Monthly Meeting and Falmouth Quarterly Meeting are part of New England Yearly Meeting (NEYM), whose website provides information about Quakers in New England, and numerous other Quaker organizations.

We, the members and attenders of Lewiston Monthly Meeting, welcome you as you are, or as you hope to be.  Our welcome includes all visitors and seekers of whatever age, ethnicity, race, sexual identity, gender identity, ability or disability, faith persuasion or none.

A fundamental belief of Quakerism is that there is that of God in everyone, every life is precious and we all have gifts to nurture and share.

You are welcome to join us in worship, and in our community of reflection and action.
